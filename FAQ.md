# FAQ: AI Use and Oversight at Turtini

This FAQ is intended for government, public sector, and regulated-industry partners.

It explains how Turtini uses artificial intelligence systems and how human oversight, accountability, and control are maintained.

---

## Is Turtini operated or managed by AI systems?

No.

Turtini is operated and managed by human leadership and staff.

All business decisions, contractual commitments, and operational accountability remain the responsibility of authorized human personnel. AI systems are used only as supporting tools under human direction.

---

## What role does AI play in Turtini’s operations?

AI systems may be used to support:
- Information analysis and research
- Drafting and summarization of technical or administrative materials
- Scenario modeling and option comparison
- Execution of limited, pre-approved, and reversible operational tasks

AI systems do not independently direct operations or determine outcomes.

---

## Does Turtini use autonomous or self-directing AI agents?

No.

Turtini does not deploy fully autonomous or self-directing AI agents.

All AI systems operate:
- Within predefined scopes
- Under explicit human authorization
- With the ability for human override, suspension, or termination at any time

---

## Can AI systems make decisions or commitments on behalf of Turtini?

No.

AI systems may generate recommendations or analysis, but:
- Final decisions are made by humans
- Legal, financial, contractual, and policy decisions require human approval
- AI systems cannot bind Turtini or its customers in any way

---

## Can AI systems represent Turtini to customers, agencies, or partners?

No.

AI systems do not act as representatives of Turtini and do not engage independently with customers, agencies, or partners.

All official communications, representations, and commitments are made by authorized human personnel.

---

## How does Turtini ensure accountability for AI-assisted actions?

All AI-assisted activities are:
- Initiated or approved by humans
- Traceable to a defined purpose and instruction
- Logged where operationally appropriate
- Subject to human review and audit

If an AI system cannot clearly explain the basis for an action, that action is considered invalid.

---

## Does the use of AI affect compliance or auditability?

No.

Turtini’s AI use is designed to support — not undermine — compliance, auditability, and transparency.

AI systems are used in ways that:
- Preserve clear chains of responsibility
- Support documentation and traceability
- Align with public sector governance expectations

---

## Is this framework a legal or contractual agreement?

No.

This document describes internal governance practices and does not replace contractual terms, regulatory obligations, or applicable laws.

---

## Why does Turtini publish this information publicly?

To promote transparency and reduce uncertainty.

Public sector and regulated environments require clarity around decision authority, accountability, and oversight. Publishing this information allows partners and customers to understand how AI is used and governed at Turtini.

---

## Will Turtini’s approach to AI change over time?

Yes, as technology and regulations evolve.

What will remain constant:
- Human accountability for decisions and outcomes
- Explicit limits on AI authority
- Oversight by design
- Commitment to long-term institutional trust
